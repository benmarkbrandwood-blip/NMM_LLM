## Why the draft was rejected

- 64 exact positions collapse to only 63 symmetry orbits.
- The selection is a deterministic, approximately five-percent slice of the
  available opening-book-derived orbits rather than a principled population
  sample.
- It has no flying starts and only 12 early movement starts.
- It inherits ambiguity from named lines whose mill-removal choices are not
  encoded.
- Calling it training-disjoint is unsupported.
- Reusing these 64 deterministic starts for 256 pairs would copy each result
  four times and understate uncertainty.

Do not regenerate this draft and treat it as accepted merely because the
builder is deterministic.

## Named-line replay audit

The Sanmill asset has 107 named lines. Exhaustive legal replay found:

| Finding | Count |

| Legal endpoints for an affected line | 2–42 |
| Lines that cannot be replayed successfully | 1 (`novel-25964b79`) |
| Successfully replayed endpoints that are already terminal | 1 (`novel-17e922b1`) |

The historical builder resolves omitted removal choices through a sorted
depth-first search. A chosen endpoint is therefore generated by the builder
rather than uniquely specified by `lineMoves`. “One line, one final
position” is not an acceptable frozen-corpus rule.

The source labels comprise 11 curated `book` records and 96 `learned`
records; the latter split into 41 `book-*` and 55 `novel-*` identifiers.
The lines also overlap the training opening pool: 100 of 107 exact move
sequences match, including all 11 curated lines. Training forces the learner's
first four placements from a sampled opening in 50% of games, but the log does
not record the selected opening identifier. Exact exposure frequency cannot be
reconstructed.
